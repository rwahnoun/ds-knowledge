Uric acid is the end-product of purine nucleotide metabolism in humans (lacking uricase, unlike most mammals). It is a weak diprotic acid (pKa1 5.4, pKa2 10.3). At urinary pH <5.5, it exists predominantly as undissociated uric acid (poorly soluble), which can crystallise and form kidney stones. At pH >6, the more soluble urate ion predominates.

### Molecules Not to Be Confused With

| Molecule | Formula | MW (g/mol) | Key Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Urea** | CH₄N₂O | 60.06 | From amino acid catabolism (urea cycle), not purine metabolism; much smaller |
| **Xanthine** | C₅H₄N₄O₂ | 152.11 | Purine precursor of uric acid; substrate of xanthine oxidase |
| **Allantoin** | C₄H₆N₄O₃ | 158.12 | Product of uricase (absent in humans); used in cosmetics |
| **Creatinine** | C₄H₇N₃O | 113.12 | From creatine metabolism; different pathway |

## Medical Information

### Origin

#### Endogenous: Purine Catabolism

Uric acid is the final product of purine degradation in humans. Pathway: Adenine/Guanine nucleotides -> hypoxanthine -> xanthine -> uric acid (catalysed by xanthine oxidase/dehydrogenase). Humans lack uricase (pseudogene), so uric acid is the end-product. Production: ~600-800 mg/day. Two-thirds from endogenous purine turnover, one-third from dietary purines.

#### Exogenous

High-purine foods: organ meats (liver, kidney), anchovies, sardines, shellfish, red meat, beer (yeast purines). Fructose and alcohol increase uric acid production.

### Primary & Secondary Biological Roles

**Primary role:**
- **Waste product:** End-product of purine catabolism for excretion.

**Secondary roles:**
- **Antioxidant:** Uric acid accounts for ~50% of plasma antioxidant capacity; scavenges peroxynitrite, hydroxyl radicals.
- **Blood pressure modulation:** Elevated uric acid associated with hypertension (debated whether causal).

### Catabolism and Elimination Pathway

- ~70% eliminated by kidneys, ~30% by GI tract (intestinal uricolysis by bacteria).
- Renal handling is complex: freely filtered -> ~90% reabsorbed (URAT1 transporter) -> ~50% secreted (OAT1/3) -> ~40% post-secretory reabsorption. Net fractional excretion: ~8-12%.
- Allopurinol and febuxostat inhibit xanthine oxidase (reduce production). Probenecid and lesinurad are uricosurics (increase excretion).

### Expression in Humans

#### Normal Levels

| Compartment | Reference Range |
|---|---|
| **Serum uric acid** | Male: 3.5-7.2 mg/dL; Female: 2.6-6.0 mg/dL |
| **Urinary uric acid (24-h)** | 250-750 mg/day (1.5-4.5 mmol/day) on regular diet |
| **Urinary uric acid (low-purine)** | <400 mg/day |

#### Factors Influencing Levels

**Increased urinary uric acid (hyperuricosuria):**
- High purine diet
- Gout with overproduction phenotype
- Tumour lysis syndrome
- Myeloproliferative/lymphoproliferative disorders
- Uricosuric drugs (probenecid, losartan)
- Fanconi syndrome
- Wilson disease

**Decreased urinary uric acid:**
- Xanthine oxidase inhibitors (allopurinol)
- CKD (reduced filtration)
- Lead nephropathy (saturine gout)
- Dehydration (reduced urine volume increases concentration but decreases total excretion)

#### Associated Pathologies

| Condition | Uric Acid Pattern | Key Symptoms |
|---|---|---|
| **Gout** | Serum >6.8 mg/dL; variable urinary | Acute monoarthritis (podagra), tophi; ~4% adults |
| **Uric acid nephrolithiasis** | High urinary uric acid + acidic pH | Radiolucent stones; ~10% of all stones |
| **Tumour lysis syndrome** | Massive hyperuricosuria + hyperuricaemia | AKI, hyperK, hyperPO4; oncology emergency |
| **Lesch-Nyhan syndrome** | Markedly elevated; HGPRT deficiency | Self-harm, dystonia, renal stones; X-linked |

### Presence in Urine

**Should it be normally present?** **Yes** -- urinary uric acid is a normal excretory product.

**Normal urinary levels:** 250-750 mg/day.

**Form in urine:** At pH <5.5: **undissociated uric acid** (C₅H₄N₄O₃, poorly soluble). At pH >6: **urate anion** (C₅H₃N₄O₃⁻, more soluble). This pH-dependent solubility is the basis for uric acid stone formation in acidic urine.

**Pathological significance:**

| Urinary Uric Acid | Possible Causes | Prevalence |
|---|---|---|
| **High (>750 mg/day)** | Overproduction, uricosurics, high-purine diet, TLS | Gout: ~4% |
| **Low (<250 mg/day)** | XO inhibitors, CKD, underexcretion gout | Common with meds |

**Solubility:** Very pH-dependent: ~60 mg/L at pH 5.0; ~200 mg/L at pH 7.0; ~1580 mg/L at pH 8.0. Uric acid crystals (rhomboid or rosette shapes, yellow-brown) are common in acidic urine.

## Detection in Urine

### Available Clinical Assays

1. **Uricase-peroxidase enzymatic assay (gold standard):**
   - **Principle:** Uricase oxidises uric acid -> allantoin + H2O2. H2O2 + peroxidase + chromogen (4-aminoantipyrine + DHBS) -> quinoneimine dye.
   - **Detection:** Absorbance at **520 nm** (Trinder) or **293 nm** (UV, direct uricase).
   - **LOD:** ~0.1 mg/dL.
   - **Advantages:** Specific, automated.

2. **UV spectrophotometry (uricase differential):**
   - **Principle:** Measure absorbance at 293 nm before and after uricase treatment. Difference = uric acid.
   - **Detection:** **293 nm** (uric acid UV absorption peak).
   - **LOD:** ~0.05 mg/dL.
   - **Advantages:** Reference method; very specific.

3. **Phosphotungstic acid (historical):**
   - **Principle:** Uric acid reduces phosphotungstate -> tungsten blue. Measured at 680-700 nm.
   - **LOD:** ~0.5 mg/dL. Non-specific (reacts with ascorbic acid, other reducing agents).

### Optimal Urine Type for Measurement

- **24-hour urine** for total excretion assessment (stone workup, gout classification).
- Acidify with HCl or use alkaline preservative to dissolve uric acid crystals.
- Refrigerate (uric acid precipitates in cold acidic urine; warm before analysis).
- Spot urine uric acid/creatinine ratio available.

### Actual Gold Standard

**Uricase-UV method** (293 nm differential) is the reference. Uricase-peroxidase colorimetric assay is the standard automated method. CV: 2-4%.

### Interferences in Measurement

| Interference | Effect | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Ascorbic acid** | Positive bias (phosphotungstic); negative (enzymatic-Trinder) | Reducing agent; inhibits peroxidase |
| **Allopurinol metabolites** | Minor interference | Oxipurinol may cross-react |
| **Haemolysis** | Positive bias | Released purines |
| **Lipaemia** | Positive bias | Light scattering |
| **Uric acid crystals** | Negative bias if not dissolved | Crystals settle; undercounting |

### Research Detection Methods

#### Spectroscopy Detection (UV-Vis / NIR)

- **UV absorption at 293 nm:** epsilon ~12,500 M-1cm-1. LOD: ~0.05 mg/dL. Specific when combined with uricase differential.
- **NIR:** Weak overtone bands. Not useful for urinary uric acid at clinical levels.

#### Fluorescence Detection

- Uric acid is weakly fluorescent (Ex 308 / Em 400 nm; very low quantum yield).
- **Enzymatic H2O2 -> Amplex Red:** Ex 571 / Em 585 nm. LOD: ~0.5 uM.
- **Carbon dot quenching:** Uric acid quenches CQD fluorescence. LOD: ~1 uM.

#### Raman Detection

| Peak (cm-1) | Assignment |
|---|---|
| **~630** | Ring deformation |
| **~1000-1010** | Ring breathing |
| **~1130** | C-N stretch |
| **~1370** | C=N stretch |
| **~1600-1640** | C=C/C=O stretch |

- Conventional Raman: LOD ~1-5 mM. Adequate for hyperuricosuric urine.
- SERS (Ag nanoparticles): LOD ~1-10 uM. Ex 532/785 nm.

#### FTIR Detection

| Band (cm-1) | Assignment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **~1680** | C=O stretch | Strong |
| **~1590** | C=C stretch | -- |
| **~1230** | C-N stretch | -- |
| **~740** | Ring deformation | -- |

- ATR-FTIR: LOD ~1-5 mM. Detectable in concentrated urine.

#### Voltammetry Detection

- Uric acid is easily oxidised electrochemically at **+0.3-0.4 V vs Ag/AgCl** on carbon electrodes.
- LOD: ~0.1-1 uM on modified electrodes (graphene, MWCNTs, boron-doped diamond).
- **Major interference:** Ascorbic acid oxidises at similar potential. Selective membranes (Nafion) or differential pulse voltammetry can resolve.
- Linear range: 1-1000 uM.
- Extensively studied for point-of-care sensors.

### Other Detection Technologies

1. **HPLC-UV (293 nm):** Reference for research. LOD: ~0.01 mg/dL. Separates uric acid from interferences.
2. **Colorimetric smartphone assay:** Uricase + chromogen on paper strip. LOD: ~0.5 mg/dL.
3. **Microfluidic sensors:** Integrated enzymatic or electrochemical detection. LOD: ~1 uM.
