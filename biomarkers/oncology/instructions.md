
# Objective
Generate a systematic review of tumor biomarkers detectable in human urine and for treatment monitoring via optical and electrochemical methods.

# Research Guidelines
1. **Biomarkers**: Include proteins (e.g., PSA, NMP22), nucleic acids (ctDNA, microRNA), extracellular vesicles (exosomes), and metabolites (e.g., NAD, [[fad|FAD]]).

2. **Technologies**:
   - Optical: Fluorescence, absorbance, and scattering
   - Electrochemistry: Voltammetry, amperometry, potentiometry, and impedance spectroscopy (EIS).

3. **Clinical Context**: For each biomarker, specify whether it is used for:
   - Initial screening/diagnosis.
   - Monitoring treatment efficacy (pharmacodynamics).
   - Detection of minimal residual disease or recurrence (remission).
   - Type of sample preparation (pre-analytical).

# Expected Format
Present the results as comparative tables including:
- **Table 1**: Biomarker name, associated cancer type, detection method, detection parameters, limit of detection (LOD) if available, level of clinical validation (validated or candidate).
- **Table 2**: Associated cancer type, biomarker type (diagnostic or monitoring), biomarker name.
- **Table 3**: An evaluation of the level of evidence based on: publication impact factor, type of protocol, number of citations, research laboratory, and number of patients.

Present the results as a matrix including, on one hand, the clinical relevance of the biomarkers and, on the other hand, the accessibility of detection by a device such as the Jimini.

# IMPORTANT:
1. **Sources**: Prioritize meta-analyses and literature reviews published between 2000 and 2026.
2. Reply only in English.
3. try first to download papers using the scihub skill
