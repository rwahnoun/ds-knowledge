# Research Plan: Urinary Tumor Biomarkers — Optical & Electrochemical Detection

## Research Question
Systematic review of tumor biomarkers detectable in human urine via optical (fluorescence, absorbance, scattering) and electrochemical (voltammetry, amperometry, EIS) methods. Covers proteins, nucleic acids, extracellular vesicles, and organic metabolites. Priority on reviews/meta-analyses 2020–2026.

## Questions
1. **Q1 — Protein biomarkers:** Which urinary protein biomarkers (PSA, NMP22, HE4, CA125, VEGF, etc.) are detectable via optical or electrochemical biosensors? What LODs are reported? What cancers and clinical stages?
2. **Q2 — Nucleic acid biomarkers:** Which ctDNA and miRNA biomarkers in urine have been detected by optical/electrochemical methods? LODs, cancer types, clinical utility?
3. **Q3 — Extracellular vesicles:** What exosome/EV-based urinary biomarkers exist? Detection platforms, LODs, clinical context?
4. **Q4 — Metabolites:** Which organic metabolites in urine serve as tumor biomarkers detectable by these methods? LODs, cancer types?
5. **Q5 — Technology comparison:** How do optical vs. electrochemical platforms compare in sensitivity, specificity, multiplexing, and clinical readiness for urinary biomarkers?
6. **Q6 — Clinical utility mapping:** For each biomarker, is it used for (a) screening/diagnosis, (b) treatment monitoring/pharmacodynamics, or (c) minimal residual disease/recurrence detection?

## Strategy
- **4 parallel researchers**, each covering a disjoint biomarker class + detection methods
  - R1: Protein biomarkers (optical + electrochemical)
  - R2: Nucleic acids — ctDNA & miRNA (optical + electrochemical)
  - R3: Extracellular vesicles / exosomes (optical + electrochemical)
  - R4: Metabolites + technology comparison reviews
- **Round 2 (if needed):** Targeted gap-filling — clinical utility mapping, missing LOD data, paper downloads
- **Expected rounds:** 1–2

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] All 4 biomarker classes covered with ≥3 independent sources each
- [ ] Comparative table with biomarker, cancer, method, LOD, clinical utility for ≥20 biomarker–method pairs
- [ ] Optical AND electrochemical methods represented for each biomarker class
- [ ] Clinical utility (diagnosis / monitoring / recurrence) mapped for major biomarkers
- [ ] Contradictions in LOD or clinical claims identified
- [ ] ≥5 key papers downloaded via Sci-Hub
- [ ] No single-source claims on critical findings
