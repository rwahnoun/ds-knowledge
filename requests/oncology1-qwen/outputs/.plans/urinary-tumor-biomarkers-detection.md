# Research Plan: urinary-tumor-biomarkers-detection

## Questions
1. What urinary tumor biomarkers are detectable via optical and electrochemical methods?
2. What are the specific optical methods (fluorescence, absorbance, scattering) and electrochemical methods (voltammetry, amperometry, EIS) used for detection?
3. For each biomarker, what is its clinical utility in screening/diagnosis, treatment monitoring, or residual disease detection?
4. What are the LOD values for these biomarkers when detected via these methods?
5. How do these methods compare in terms of sensitivity and specificity?

## Strategy
- Researcher allocations and dimensions:
  - Biomarker type researcher (proteins, nucleic acids, exosomes, metabolites)
  - Optical methods researcher (fluorescence, absorbance, scattering)
  - Electrochemical methods researcher (voltammetry, amperometry, EIS)
  - Clinical utility researcher (screening/diagnosis, treatment monitoring, residual disease detection)
- Expected rounds: 2-3 research rounds

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] All key questions answered with ≥2 independent sources
- [ ] Contradictions identified and addressed
- [ ] No single-source claims on critical findings
- [ ] LOD data available for at least 5 biomarkers
- [ ] Clinical utility clearly mapped to each biomarker
